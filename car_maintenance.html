What is a VIN or vehicle identification number?


The Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) is a distinctive 17-character code assigned by manufacturers to each automobile, functioning as an automotive equivalent to a social security number. It provides comprehensive information about the vehicle, including details like year, make, model, engine size, manufacturer, manufacturing location, and safety features. Beyond specifying vehicle specifications, a VIN can be employed to identify recalls, registrations, warranty claims, insurance coverage, and theft records.


Why is checking a VIN on a used car important?


When contemplating the purchase of a pre-owned vehicle, it's crucial to inspect the car's VIN. This examination aids in assessing the quality and reliability of the used car. Inconsistencies between the advertised VIN and the vehicle's make and model may signal a potential scam or unauthorized modifications. Employing a standard third-party VIN check can further unveil essential vehicle history details, such as reports of theft or a clean title.


How to find your car's VIN number?


Search for your car's VIN on the front of the dashboard, situated on the driver's side. The VIN is most visible from outside the vehicle, viewed through the windshield, in the space where the hood meets the windshield. Alternatively, locate the VIN on the driver's side door post. This area is visible when you open the door and inspect the region near where the door latches to the car. If the VIN is not readily found on the car, it should be printed on your insurance cards and your vehicle's title.
